When adolescent cows reach 900 pounds, they begin consuming a formulated
diet of vitamins, minerals, grains and feed. This allows cows to grow muscle
efficiently, expediting the slaughtering process. Nutrients existing to preserve
the diet contain nitrogen and phosphorus, which are essential for plant and
animal nourishment. However, the overabundance of nutrients flushed into US
rivers can be detrimental in health and ecological factors.
